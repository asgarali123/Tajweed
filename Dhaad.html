Letter Daad (ض) - Tajweed Rules

This video explains the proper pronunciation of the letter Daad (ض).

Introduction to the Letter Daad (ض)

Daad is a unique Arabic letter with a distinct articulation point, making it one of the more challenging letters for learners to master.

Makhraj (Articulation Point) of Daad

The articulation point of the letter (ض) is from the sides of the tongue, which makes it unique.

  • The sides of the tongue press against the upper molars.
  • It requires a special muscular effort as it is not commonly used in daily speech.

Sifaat (Characteristics)

  • Isti'laa: Elevation of the tongue.
  • Itbaq: The tongue creates a seal with the roof of the mouth.
  • Jahr: Sound is held and not whispered.
  • Shiddah: Complete stoppage of sound.

Common Mistakes When Pronouncing Daad

  • Incorrect positioning of the tongue against the molars.
  • Mixing up the sound with the letter د (Daal).
  • Lack of proper tongue elevation.

Tajweed Rules for Daad

  • Mustafilah: If before the ض there is a light letter, the sound remains light.
  • Ikhfaa Shafawi: When merging with other letters, apply ghunnah as required.

Note: Always follow your teacher’s guidance for correct application.
