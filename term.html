Tajweed Terms and Descriptions

Tajweed Terms and Descriptions

Term Description
Aaridhah A temporary quality or characteristic of a letter.
Adnal Halq The closest part of the throat used for articulating certain letters.
Aqsal Halq The farthest part of the throat used for the articulation of certain letters.
Awwal Referring to the first or beginning.
Basmalah The phrase "Bismillah ir-Rahman ir-Raheem," meaning "In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful."
Dhaatiyah An inherent characteristic or quality of a letter that is permanent.
Dhamma The short vowel "u" sound in Arabic.
Fasl A pause or separation between two words or parts of a sentence.
Fathah The short vowel "a" sound in Arabic.
Ghair Mutadhaaddah Non-contrasting or non-opposite qualities.
Ghair Other or different.
Ghunnah The nasal sound that accompanies certain letters, especially the noon and meem when they are saakinah.
Hams A soft whispering sound, often associated with letters like "ha" (ح).
Hasan Good or proper.
Huruful Madd The letters of elongation in Arabic, such as "alif" (ا), "waw" (و), and "yaa" (ي).
Idghaam Merging two letters together in recitation, typically when a saakinah noon or tanween is followed by certain letters.
Idhtirari Forced or involuntary.
Ikhfa The hiding of a letter sound, when a noon saakinah or tanween is followed by certain letters with a partial nasal sound.
Ikhtibari Temporary or trial-based.
Ikhtiyari Optional or chosen.
Infiraaf The deviation or bending of the tongue when pronouncing certain letters, especially in the articulation of certain sounds.
Infitah The opening or separation of the vocal tract when pronouncing certain letters.
Intizhari Waiting or anticipation.
Iqlab Changing the sound of a letter (e.g., turning the noon saakinah into a meem sound when followed by a baa).
Ishmaam The addition of a slight nasal sound during certain stops.
Iskaan The act of making a letter saakin, or without a vowel.
Ismaat The act of stopping or holding a sound at a certain point.
Isti’la The lifting of the tongue, typically when pronouncing heavy letters.
Istiaadha Seeking refuge, often saying "A'udhu billahi min ash-shaytan ir-rajim" before reciting the Qur'an.
Istifaal The lowering of the tongue, typically when pronouncing light letters.
Istitaalah The elongation or stretching of the sound, especially when a letter is held longer than usual.
Itbaaq The complete closure or joining of the vocal tract when pronouncing certain letters.
Izhar The clear pronunciation of a letter without merging or blending it with another letter, especially when it comes after a saakinah noon.
Izhlaaq The smooth flowing or gliding of a sound when pronouncing a letter.
Jahr A forceful or loud pronunciation of a sound, often associated with letters like "khaa" (خ).
Jowf The cavity in the throat where certain sounds are articulated.
Kafi Sufficient or adequate.
Kasrah The short vowel "i" sound in Arabic.
Kul A term for "all," often used to refer to a complete or final sound.
Laazimah An obligatory sound, one that must be pronounced distinctly.
Leen Softness in the pronunciation of certain letters, such as the vowels "alif" (ا) and "waw" (و).
Maad The elongation of a vowel sound, typically the "madd" sounds in Arabic.
Mahalliyah A contextual or situational quality of a letter that changes depending on its position.
Makhraj al Huruf The articulation points of the Arabic letters, where each letter is pronounced.
Meem Saakinah A meem (م) that has no vowel sound, making it saakinah.
Mutadhaaddah Opposites or contrasting qualities.
Noon Saakinah A noon (ن) that has no vowel (a short vowel sound).
Qabeeh Improper or unpleasant.
Qalqalah The echoing or bouncing sound that occurs when certain letters (ق, ط, ب, ج, د) are pronounced with a saakinah state.
Raum A slight vocalization or whispering of the final sound when pausing.
Rikhwat Softness or gentleness in the pronunciation of certain sounds.
Safeer The whistling sound produced when certain letters, such as س, ش, and ص, are pronounced.
Saktah A brief pause or cessation of sound, often marked during recitation.
Shiddat Intensity or forcefulness in the pronunciation of certain sounds.
Sifaat al Huruf The qualities or characteristics of the Arabic letters, describing their sounds.
Sifaat The qualities or characteristics that describe how each letter is pronounced.
Taam Complete and final in a stop.
Tafashshee A softening of a sound, such as in certain letters where the sound is spread.
Tafkheem The process of making a letter sound heavy, especially when the "ra" or "saad" is pronounced with fullness.
Tajweed The set of rules that govern the pronunciation of Arabic letters in the Qur'an to ensure proper recitation.
Takreer The rolling sound made by repeating a letter, especially the "ra" (ر).
Tanween The nasal sound added to a word, represented by a doubling of the vowel marks (e.g., fat-haan, dhammaan, kasraan).
Tarqiq The opposite of tafkheem, making a letter sound light, as in the pronunciation of certain letters.
Thani Referring to the second.
Waqf bil Ishmaam A stop that is accompanied by a slight nasal sound.
Waqf bil Iskaan A stop where the final letter is pronounced in a still state.
Waqf bil Raum A stop with a slight vocalization of the final letter.
Waqf Hasan A good or proper stop.
Waqf Idhtirari A forced or involuntary stop, due to a recitation need.
Waqf Ikhtibari A temporary stop, indicating an assessment or trial.
Waqf Ikhtiyari An optional stop, where reciters may choose to pause or continue.
Waqf Intizhari A pause that is meant for waiting or anticipation.
Waqf Kafi A sufficient or adequate stop.
Waqf Qabeeh An improper or unpleasant stop.
Waqf Taam A complete and final stop, indicating no continuation.
Waqf A pause or stop during recitation, often marked by a symbol.
Wasl The connection or joining of two words or parts in a sentence.
Wastul Halq The middle part of the throat used for articulating certain letters.
